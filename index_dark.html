3. Level/year at which this course is offered: 2nd year/ 4th level

4. Course General Description:
This course examines the ethical considerations that arise in human-computer interaction, focusing on moral principles, privacy, intellectual property, and social responsibilities. Students will analyze ethical dilemmas and frameworks, explore inclusivity and accessibility, and evaluate the societal impacts of HCI practices and emerging technologies.

5. Pre-requirements for this course: None

6. Co-requisites for this course: None

7. Course Main Objective(s):

  • Understand the ethical principles and frameworks applicable to HCI.
  • Analyze ethical dilemmas in technology and propose informed solutions.
  • Appreciate the importance of inclusivity, privacy, and security in HCI design.
  • Explore the societal impacts of emerging technologies and online ecosystems.
  • Develop critical thinking and ethical reasoning skills to navigate professional challenges in HCI.
